The reading section<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

There are three passages (total 20*3 = 40 questions) in the IELTS Reading test. You will have a total of 60 minutes to complete this reading section.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

You MUST write your answers quickly and directly on the answer sheet because there will be no extra time for transferring your answers to the sheet.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The content<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

For IELTS academic tests: the texts are about academic subjects taken from books or journals. Its knowledge will not be too specialized but not too simple.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

For the IELTS General test<\/a>: the texts are likely informal and close to daily life such as leaflets and magazines. It uses more familiar words rather than academic ones.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Reading tips<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
    \n
  • Use your time effectively. Don\u2019t spend too much time solving a question (you should move on if it took more than 3 minutes)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • DO NOT read the entire passage. Read the title, and subtitle of the passage to have a quick general look at the passage.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Focus on what the question asks, and what types of them<\/a> and use skimming and scanning techniques to answer them.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Do the questions which ask about detailed information FIRST, and do the questions which ask general summary LAST.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Skimming<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

    Imagine you are going to buy a newspaper at the shop. You cannot read the entire content of it to decide whether to buy or not.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Skimming is to understand the general meaning of the text, you should read a newspaper quickly every day to practice skimming.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
